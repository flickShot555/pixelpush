1. Overview
This document is the single source of truth for all pricing at PixelPush. It is split into two parts:
what Aepostrophee pays to run the product (outgoing costs), and what users pay to use the
product (incoming revenue). Both sides are broken down across all growth stages — from pre-
launch to scale — so financial expectations are clear at every milestone.
How to Read This Document
Part A covers your costs — infrastructure, APIs, and transaction fees. Part B covers user-facing
pricing — the free tier, Pro subscription, Lifetime Deal, and all revenue scenarios. Part C brings both
sides together into a net profit view.

9. Pricing Decision Guide
This section captures key pricing decisions and the reasoning behind each one, for future
reference when revisiting or adjusting the pricing model.
Decision Rationale
Why $7/month for Pro? Positioned below the $10 psychological threshold. Cheap enough
to be an impulse purchase for a developer, meaningful enough to
generate real MRR. Comparable to a domain name or one coffee
per month.
Why $35 for Lifetime? Exactly 5 months of Pro. Positioned as 'buy once, save forever'
for early believers. High enough to generate meaningful launch
revenue, low enough to convert hesitant users.
Why cap the Lifetime Deal? Unlimited lifetime deals eventually cannibalize subscription
revenue. A cap of 200–500 creates urgency, limits long-term
revenue dilution, and keeps the core model subscription-based.
Why not charge more for Pro? Developer tools in this category (profile tools, productivity apps)
typically price at $5–10/month. PixelPush is a fun creative tool,
not a mission-critical utility. Volume at $7 beats lower volume at
$15.
Why is AI suggestions a Pro
feature?
It has a real per-call cost (Claude API), it delivers career value
worth paying for, and it is technically differentiated. It is the feature
most likely to convert free users to Pro.
Why no annual plan at launch? Annual plans reduce churn but lock in revenue at a discount and
add billing complexity. Launch with monthly only and introduce an
annual option (e.g. $60/year = 2 months free) once monthly
retention data is established.

10. Glossary
Term Definition
MRR Monthly Recurring Revenue. Total monthly income from all active Pro
subscribers.
Net MRR MRR after Stripe transaction fees are deducted.
Gross Margin Revenue minus direct costs, expressed as a percentage of revenue. For software,
this excludes infrastructure and API costs.
LTD Lifetime Deal. A one-time payment for permanent access to a subscription
product.
Conversion Rate The percentage of free users who upgrade to a paid plan.
Churn The percentage of paid subscribers who cancel in a given month. Low churn is
critical for healthy MRR growth.
ARPU Average Revenue Per User. Total MRR divided by total paying users. For
PixelPush Pro, ARPU is $7 minus Stripe fees = $6.50.
Break-Even The number of paying subscribers required for monthly revenue to equal monthly
infrastructure costs.
CAC Customer Acquisition Cost. What it costs on average to acquire one paying
customer. For PixelPush, viral sharing is the primary channel so CAC is near
zero.
LTV Lifetime Value. The total net revenue expected from a single subscriber over their
lifetime. At $6.50/month net with average 6-month retention, LTV = ~$39.
